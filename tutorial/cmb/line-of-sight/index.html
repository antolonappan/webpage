The line-of-sight integral

In principle the Boltzmann hierarchy of chapter 3 gives us the photon distribution Θℓ(k⃗,τ0)\Theta_\ell(\vec k, \tau_0)Θℓ​(k,τ0​) at the observer for every ℓ\ellℓ and k⃗\vec kk. In practice this is hopeless: to get to ℓmax≈2500\ell_\mathrm{max} \approx 2500ℓmax​≈2500 we would need to evolve thousands of multipoles per mode, with each multipole receiving streaming power from below. The numerical cost is prohibitive.

Seljak and Zaldarriaga (1996) found a way out. Their observation is that the Boltzmann equation can be written in integral form, with the streaming part of the operator turned into a spherical Bessel function, leaving only the source terms – scattering, monopole, Doppler, ISW – to be computed dynamically. Then we evolve the hierarchy only to the few low multipoles needed to build the sources (typically ℓ≤3\ell \leq 3ℓ≤3), and project them onto the sphere with a line-of-sight integral. This chapter derives that integral and constructs the source function.

Spherical-harmonic toolbox

The geometry of the projection from k⃗\vec kk-space to the sky requires three identities. They are standard and we collect them here for reference.

Plane wave expansion.

A plane wave with wavevector k⃗\vec kk expanded in spherical harmonics about an origin:

eik⃗⋅x⃗=4π∑ℓmiℓ jℓ(kr) Yℓm∗(k^) Yℓm(x^).e^{i\vec k \cdot \vec x} = 4\pi \sum_{\ell m} i^\ell\, j_\ell(kr)\, Y^*_{\ell m}(\hat k)\, Y_{\ell m}(\hat x).eik⋅x=4πℓm∑​iℓjℓ​(kr)Yℓm∗​(k^)Yℓm​(x^).

Equivalently, in the form we will use:

eik⃗⋅x⃗=∑ℓ(2ℓ+1) iℓ jℓ(kr) Pℓ(k^⋅x^).e^{i\vec k \cdot \vec x} = \sum_\ell (2\ell + 1)\, i^\ell\, j_\ell(kr)\, P_\ell(\hat k \cdot \hat x).eik⋅x=ℓ∑​(2ℓ+1)iℓjℓ​(kr)Pℓ​(k^⋅x^).

Spherical Bessel functions.

Defined by

jℓ(x)=π2x Jℓ+1/2(x).j_\ell(x) = \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2 x}}\, J_{\ell + 1/2}(x).jℓ​(x)=2xπ​​Jℓ+1/2​(x).

For small argument jℓ(x)∼xℓ/(2ℓ+1)!!j_\ell(x) \sim x^\ell/(2\ell + 1)!!jℓ​(x)∼xℓ/(2ℓ+1)!!; for large argument jℓ(x)∼x−1sin⁡(x−ℓπ/2)j_\ell(x) \sim x^{-1}\sin(x - \ell\pi/2)jℓ​(x)∼x−1sin(x−ℓπ/2). Near x=ℓx = \ellx=ℓ there is a peak and the function transitions from exponentially suppressed to oscillatory. This sharp localisation in k⋅rk \cdot rk⋅r near ℓ/r\ell/rℓ/r is what makes the Limber approximation (chapter 6) work and what allows us to localise transfer-function support in kkk.

The recurrence we will use repeatedly:

jℓ′(x)=ℓx jℓ(x)−jℓ+1(x),jℓ′′(x)=−2x jℓ′(x)+(ℓ(ℓ+1)x2−1) jℓ(x).j_\ell'(x) = \frac{\ell}{x}\, j_\ell(x) - j_{\ell + 1}(x), \qquad j_\ell''(x) = -\frac{2}{x}\, j_\ell'(x) + \Bigl(\frac{\ell(\ell+1)}{x^2} - 1\Bigr)\, j_\ell(x).jℓ′​(x)=xℓ​jℓ​(x)−jℓ+1​(x),jℓ′′​(x)=−x2​jℓ′​(x)+(x2ℓ(ℓ+1)​−1)jℓ​(x).

Addition theorem.

Pℓ(k^⋅n^)=4π2ℓ+1∑mYℓm(k^) Yℓm∗(n^).P_\ell(\hat k \cdot \hat n) = \frac{4\pi}{2\ell + 1}\sum_m Y_{\ell m}(\hat k)\, Y^*_{\ell m}(\hat n).Pℓ​(k^⋅n^)=2ℓ+14π​m∑​Yℓm​(k^)Yℓm∗​(n^).

This is how we move from the k⃗\vec kk-axis frame (where the Boltzmann hierarchy lives) to the sky frame (where we project onto YℓmY_{\ell m}Yℓm​ harmonics).

From the Boltzmann hierarchy to an integral

We work with the conformal time variable τ\tauτ along a photon trajectory from last scattering to today. A photon arriving at the observer from direction n^\hat nn^ at time τ0\tau_0τ0​ has, at the earlier time τ\tauτ, the spatial position x⃗(τ)=(τ0−τ) n^\vec x(\tau) = (\tau_0 - \tau)\,\hat nx(τ)=(τ0​−τ)n^. Write χ(τ)=τ0−τ\chi(\tau) = \tau_0 - \tauχ(τ)=τ0​−τ for the comoving distance back to that time.

The Boltzmann equation for the temperature anisotropy along a photon path is

ddτ(δT/T)+κ˙ (δT/T)=ST(x⃗,n^,τ),\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}\tau}(\delta T/T) + \dot\kappa\,(\delta T/T) = S^T(\vec x, \hat n, \tau),dτd​(δT/T)+κ˙(δT/T)=ST(x,n^,τ),

where the source STS^TST collects all non-streaming terms: Thomson scattering off electrons (which feeds in Θ0+μvb+12P2(μ)Π\Theta_0 + \mu v_b + \tfrac{1}{2}P_2(\mu)\PiΘ0​+μvb​+21​P2​(μ)Π from the monopole, Doppler, and polarisation-quadrupole contributions), the gravitational redshift Φ˙+μ2h˙/2\dot\Phi + \mu^2\dot h/2Φ˙+μ2h˙/2 (the ISW + Sachs-Wolfe terms), and so on. The integrating factor for Eq. is e−κ(τ)e^{-\kappa(\tau)}e−κ(τ), and integrating gives the formal solution

(δT/T)(n^,τ0)=∫0τ0ST(x⃗(τ),n^,τ) e−κ(τ) dτ.(\delta T/T)(\hat n, \tau_0) = \int_0^{\tau_0} S^T(\vec x(\tau), \hat n, \tau)\, e^{-\kappa(\tau)}\,\mathrm{d}\tau.(δT/T)(n^,τ0​)=∫0τ0​​ST(x(τ),n^,τ)e−κ(τ)dτ.

The factor e−κ(τ)e^{-\kappa(\tau)}e−κ(τ) is the probability that a photon survived from τ\tauτ to today without re-scattering. This is the line-of-sight form: an integral over conformal time of a source weighted by the surviving fraction.

Going to k⃗\vec kk-space and projecting onto multipoles

Fourier transform Eq., expand eik⃗⋅x⃗(τ)=eikχ(τ) μe^{i\vec k\cdot \vec x(\tau)} = e^{i k\chi(\tau)\,\mu}eik⋅x(τ)=eikχ(τ)μ using Eq., and project onto multipoles using the addition theorem. After lengthy but standard algebra the result is the line-of-sight integral for the temperature transfer function:

ΔℓT(k)=∫0τ0 dτ [S~0T jℓ(kχ)+S~1T jℓ′(kχ)+S~2T jℓ′′(kχ)],\Delta^T_\ell(k) = \int_0^{\tau_0}\,\mathrm{d}\tau\,\bigl[\widetilde S^T_0\, j_\ell(k\chi) + \widetilde S^T_1\, j_\ell'(k\chi) + \widetilde S^T_2\, j_\ell''(k\chi)\bigr],ΔℓT​(k)=∫0τ0​​dτ[S0T​jℓ​(kχ)+S1T​jℓ′​(kχ)+S2T​jℓ′′​(kχ)],

with χ=τ0−τ\chi = \tau_0 - \tauχ=τ0​−τ, and where the three source components are

S~0T=2 Ψ˙W e−κ+g (Θ0+ΨW+58Π),S~1T=g (σ+vb),S~2T=158 g Π.\begin{aligned} \widetilde S^T_0 &= 2\, \dot\Psi_W\, e^{-\kappa} + g\,(\Theta_0 + \Psi_W + \tfrac{5}{8}\Pi), \\ \widetilde S^T_1 &= g\,(\sigma + v_b), \\ \widetilde S^T_2 &= \tfrac{15}{8}\, g\, \Pi. \end{aligned}S0T​S1T​S2T​​=2Ψ˙W​e−κ+g(Θ0​+ΨW​+85​Π),=g(σ+vb​),=815​gΠ.​

Here g(τ)=κ˙ e−κg(\tau) = \dot\kappa\,e^{-\kappa}g(τ)=κ˙e−κ is the visibility function from chapter 2, and Π=(πγ+9E2/5)/10\Pi = (\pi_\gamma + 9 E_2/5)/10Π=(πγ​+9E2​/5)/10 is the polarisation source. The three terms in S~0T\widetilde S^T_0S0T​ correspond to the integrated Sachs-Wolfe effect (ISW), the Sachs-Wolfe + intrinsic temperature contribution at last scattering, and a small quadrupolar contribution to the temperature monopole. The S~1T\widetilde S^T_1S1T​ piece is the Doppler effect: photons scatter off baryons whose peculiar velocity is vbv_bvb​. The S~2T\widetilde S^T_2S2T​ piece is the quadrupole-induced polarisation feedback.

Why three Bessel channels?

The original Seljak-Zaldarriaga source had a single Bessel function jℓj_\elljℓ​ multiplied by a long expression. Integration by parts on the v˙b\dot v_bv˙b​ and Π˙\dot\PiΠ˙ terms moves the time derivatives off the source and onto the kernel, generating jℓ′(kχ)j_\ell'(k\chi)jℓ′​(kχ) and jℓ′′(kχ)j_\ell''(k\chi)jℓ′′​(kχ). The boundary terms vanish because g(τ)g(\tau)g(τ) is zero outside recombination/reionisation. The advantage is that S~0T,S~1T,S~2T\widetilde S^T_0, \widetilde S^T_1, \widetilde S^T_2S0T​,S1T​,S2T​ are all built from low-ℓ\ellℓ photon multipoles (Θ0\Theta_0Θ0​, Θ2\Theta_2Θ2​, E2E_2E2​) plus metric variables; we do not need the full hierarchy.

The EEE-mode source

For polarisation a similar derivation, with spin-weighted spherical harmonics in place of ordinary YℓmY_{\ell m}Yℓm​, gives

ΔℓE(k)=(ℓ+2)!/(ℓ−2)! ∫0τ0 dτ S~E jℓ(kχ)(kχ)2,\Delta^E_\ell(k) = \sqrt{(\ell + 2)!/(\ell - 2)!}\,\int_0^{\tau_0}\,\mathrm{d}\tau\,\widetilde S^E\, \frac{j_\ell(k\chi)}{(k\chi)^2},ΔℓE​(k)=(ℓ+2)!/(ℓ−2)!​∫0τ0​​dτSE(kχ)2jℓ​(kχ)​,

with the EEE-mode source

S~E=158 g Π.\widetilde S^E = \frac{15}{8}\, g\, \Pi.SE=815​gΠ.

The geometric prefactor (ℓ+2)!/(ℓ−2)!≈ℓ2\sqrt{(\ell+2)!/(\ell-2)!} \approx \ell^2(ℓ+2)!/(ℓ−2)!​≈ℓ2 for large ℓ\ellℓ comes from the differential operator that turns the spin-2 polarisation field into the scalar EEE mode. The 1/(kχ)21/(k\chi)^21/(kχ)2 is the dual Bessel-function factor that makes Eq. the correct projection of a spin-2 source.

Source-function decomposition in the code

To match the numerical structure of Eq., our code returns four arrays per (k,τ)(k, \tau)(k,τ) pair:

src_j0=S~0T,src_j1=S~1T,src_j2=S~2T,src_E=S~E/(χ2k2).\texttt{src\_j0} = \widetilde S^T_0,\qquad \texttt{src\_j1} = \widetilde S^T_1,\qquad \texttt{src\_j2} = \widetilde S^T_2,\qquad \texttt{src\_E} = \widetilde S^E\big/\bigl(\chi^2 k^2\bigr).src_j0=S0T​,src_j1=S1T​,src_j2=S2T​,src_E=SE/(χ2k2).

The 1/(χ2k2)1/(\chi^2 k^2)1/(χ2k2) factor in src_E absorbs the 1/(kχ)21/(k\chi)^21/(kχ)2 in Eq. ahead of time, so the LOS integral simply convolves src_E with jℓj_\elljℓ​ – no separate Bessel-derivative book-keeping for the EEE mode.

Building the source function: gravitational potentials

The synchronous-gauge code from chapter 3 carries η\etaη and hhh, not Φ\PhiΦ and Ψ\PsiΨ. To use the line-of-sight formula we need to reconstruct the Newtonian-gauge metric variables, or equivalently the Weyl potential ΨW\Psi_WΨW​. The relation in synchronous gauge is

ΨW=−12k2[(8πGa2δρ+3H8πGa2(ρ+p)θ/k)+8πGa2(ρ+p)σanis],\Psi_W = -\frac{1}{2 k^2}\bigl[(8\pi G a^2\delta\rho + 3 \mathcal{H} 8\pi G a^2 (\rho + p)\theta/k) + 8\pi G a^2 (\rho + p)\sigma_\text{anis}\bigr],ΨW​=−2k21​[(8πGa2δρ+3H8πGa2(ρ+p)θ/k)+8πGa2(ρ+p)σanis​],

where the bracketed combination is the same one that enters the Einstein 00-equation. Its time derivative Ψ˙W\dot\Psi_WΨ˙W​, which sources the ISW effect, can be evaluated from the Boltzmann hierarchy without re-evaluating the full RHS – we have all the ingredients (π˙γ\dot\pi_\gammaπ˙γ​, π˙ν\dot\pi_\nuπ˙ν​, σ˙\dot\sigmaσ˙) already.

The code: build_sources

Cell: build_sources 
def build_sources(tau, y, k, pgrid, thermo):
    """Source function building blocks at a single (k, tau) point.

    Returns (ISW, monopole, sigma+vb, vis, polter, source_E) -- the
    raw pieces that get combined into the three temperature Bessel
    channels and the E-mode source.
    """
    (a, adotoa, grhog_t, grhor_t, grhoc_t, grhob_t,
     dgrho, dgq, z, sigma,
     etak, clxc, clxb, vb, clxg, qg, pig, clxr, qr, pir) = _common_terms(
        tau, y, k, pgrid['bg_vec'], pgrid['sp_a_x'], pgrid['sp_a_c'])
    opacity = max(float(_cubic_eval(pgrid['sp_op_x'], pgrid['sp_op_c'], tau)),
                  1e-30)
    k2 = k * k

    E2 = y[IX_POL] if LMAXPOL >= 2 else 0.0

    # --- Weyl potential phi = (Phi + Psi)/2 in synchronous gauge ---
    dgpi = grhog_t * pig + grhor_t * pir
    phi  = -((dgrho + 3.0 * dgq * adotoa / k) + dgpi) / (2.0 * k2)

    # --- Compute phi_dot directly from hierarchy equations ---
    polter = pig / 10.0 + 9.0 / 15.0 * E2
    Theta3 = y[IX_G + 3] if LMAXG >= 3 else 0.0
    N3     = y[IX_R + 3] if LMAXNR >= 3 else 0.0
    pigdot = (2*k/5*qg - 3*k/5*Theta3 - opacity*(pig - polter) + 8*k*sigma/15)
    pirdot = (2*k/5*qr - 3*k/5*N3 + 8*k*sigma/15)
    pidot_sum  = grhog_t * pigdot + grhor_t * pirdot
    diff_rhopi = pidot_sum - 4.0 * adotoa * dgpi
    gpres_plus_grho = (4.0/3.0) * (grhog_t + grhor_t) + grhoc_t + grhob_t
    phidot = 0.5 * (adotoa * (-dgpi - 2.0 * k2 * phi) + dgq * k
                     - diff_rhopi + k * sigma * gpres_plus_grho) / k2

    # --- Visibility and exp(-tau) at this time ---
    tau_min, tau_max = thermo['tau_arr'][0], thermo['tau_arr'][-1]
    if tau < tau_min or tau > tau_max:
        vis    = 0.0
        exptau = np.exp(-thermo['tau_optical'][0]) if tau < tau_min else 1.0
    else:
        vis    = float(thermo['visibility_interp'](tau))
        exptau = float(thermo['exptau_interp'](tau))

    # --- Three source components ---
    ISW       = 2.0 * phidot * exptau                       # 2 phi_dot e^{-kappa}
    monopole  = -etak / k + 2.0 * phi + clxg / 4.0          # Theta_0 + Psi_W + (3/8) delta_b...
    chi       = pgrid['tau0'] - tau
    source_E  = 15.0 / 8.0 * vis * polter / (chi**2 * k2) if chi > 0 else 0.0

    return ISW, monopole, sigma + vb, vis, polter, source_E

Walking through the function.

  • phi is the Weyl potential ΨW\Psi_WΨW​ computed from Eq.: the combination of δρ\delta\rhoδρ, (ρ+p)θ(\rho + p)\theta(ρ+p)θ, and anisotropic stress that appears in the Einstein 00-equation.

  • phidot is its time derivative, computed directly from the hierarchy equations – this avoids re-evaluating the full Boltzmann RHS just to get Ψ˙W\dot{\Psi}_WΨ˙W​.

  • ISW = 2 phidot exptau is the integrated Sachs-Wolfe contribution to S~0T\widetilde S^T_0S0T​.

  • monopole = -etak/k + 2 phi + clxg/4 is the Sachs-Wolfe combination Θ0+ΨW\Theta_0 + \Psi_WΘ0​+ΨW​ in synchronous gauge.

  • sigma + vb is the Doppler source S~1T\widetilde S^T_1S1T​ apart from the visibility prefactor.

  • polter is the polarisation source Π=πγ/10+9E2/15\Pi = \pi_\gamma/10 + 9 E_2/15Π=πγ​/10+9E2​/15.

  • source_E is S~E/(kχ)2=(15/8) g Π/(kχ)2\widetilde S^E/(k\chi)^2 = (15/8)\, g\, \Pi/(k\chi)^2SE/(kχ)2=(15/8)gΠ/(kχ)2, ready for the LOS integral.

The caller (evolve_k in the next chapter) assembles these into the three temperature channels

src_j0=ISW+vis (monopole+58polter),src_j1=vis (σ+vb),src_j2=158 vis polter.\texttt{src\_j0} = \texttt{ISW} + \texttt{vis}\,(\texttt{monopole} + \tfrac{5}{8}\texttt{polter}), \quad \texttt{src\_j1} = \texttt{vis}\,(\sigma + v_b), \quad \texttt{src\_j2} = \tfrac{15}{8}\,\texttt{vis}\,\texttt{polter}.src_j0=ISW+vis(monopole+85​polter),src_j1=vis(σ+vb​),src_j2=815​vispolter.

Updated evolve_k: also return source functions

Our evolve_k from chapter 3 returns the full perturbation state. For the LOS step we want source functions at each output time. Extend the function to return both.

Cell: evolve_k (source-function version) 
def evolve_k(k, bg, thermo, pgrid, tau_out):
    """Evolve perturbations for wavenumber k, return source functions on tau_out.
    Returns (src_j0, src_j1, src_j2, src_E)."""
    tau_start = min(0.01 / k, tau_out[0] * 0.5)
    tau_start = max(tau_start, 0.1)
    y0 = adiabatic_ics(k, tau_start, bg, pgrid)

    bg_vec  = pgrid['bg_vec']
    sp_a_x  = pgrid['sp_a_x'];  sp_a_c  = pgrid['sp_a_c']
    sp_op_x = pgrid['sp_op_x']; sp_op_c = pgrid['sp_op_c']
    sp_cs_x = pgrid['sp_cs_x']; sp_cs_c = pgrid['sp_cs_c']

    sol = integrate.solve_ivp(
        lambda tau, y: boltzmann_derivs(
            tau, y, k, bg_vec, sp_a_x, sp_a_c, sp_op_x, sp_op_c, sp_cs_x, sp_cs_c),
        [tau_start, tau_out[-1]], y0, t_eval=tau_out,
        method='LSODA', rtol=1e-5, atol=1e-8, max_step=20.0)
    if not sol.success:
        raise RuntimeError(f"ODE solver failed for k={k:.4e}: {sol.message}")

    ntau = len(tau_out)
    ISW_arr      = np.zeros(ntau)
    monopole_arr = np.zeros(ntau)
    sigma_vb_arr = np.zeros(ntau)
    vis_arr      = np.zeros(ntau)
    polter_arr   = np.zeros(ntau)
    src_E        = np.zeros(ntau)
    for i, tau in enumerate(tau_out):
        (ISW_arr[i], monopole_arr[i], sigma_vb_arr[i],
         vis_arr[i], polter_arr[i], src_E[i]) = build_sources(
            tau, sol.y[:, i], k, pgrid, thermo)

    # Assemble the three temperature channels
    src_j0 = ISW_arr + vis_arr * (monopole_arr + 0.625 * polter_arr)
    src_j1 = vis_arr * sigma_vb_arr
    src_j2 = 1.875 * vis_arr * polter_arr
    return src_j0, src_j1, src_j2, src_E

Transfer functions Δℓ(k)\Delta_\ell(k)Δℓ​(k)

Once the source functions are in hand for every kkk, the LOS integral Eq. convolves them with jℓ,jℓ′,jℓ′′j_\ell, j_\ell', j_\ell''jℓ​,jℓ′​,jℓ′′​ to give ΔℓT(k)\Delta_\ell^T(k)ΔℓT​(k). The transfer function ΔℓT(k)\Delta_\ell^T(k)ΔℓT​(k) is a peaky, oscillating function of kkk – the projection of acoustic oscillations through a Bessel kernel.

A schematic example, illustrating the qualitative structure: Δℓ(k)\Delta_\ell(k)Δℓ​(k) has a Bessel-localised peak near k≈ℓ/χ∗k \approx \ell/\chi_*k≈ℓ/χ∗​ (this is the Limber-scale projection), and the wings show acoustic oscillations modulated by the Silk damping envelope.

Looking ahead

We now have everything needed for the line-of-sight integral: source-function building blocks per (k,τ)(k, \tau)(k,τ). The remaining work is bookkeeping and numerical efficiency: pick the right grids in kkk and τ\tauτ, do the LOS integral for every ℓ\ellℓ, integrate against the primordial power spectrum.
